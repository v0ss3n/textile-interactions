## Textile interactions in a day

Textile ambient interactions starting at 00:00 3 March - 00:00 4 March

- getting in bed, under blanket, leaning back against pillow, then changing the position of the pillow and placing head against pillow; rolling in bed from side to side, hugging other pillow (sometimes), shaking up pillow, pulling blanket from foot of the bed towards my head, lying on my mattress,twisting my blanket, pressing with my cheeks into my pillow
- getting up: folding the blanket away, stepping on the carpet on the side of my bed, walking over another blanket and walking over it to the bathroom
- bathroom: take of t shirt, shower curtain closing; shower curtain opening and shaking out water; step on bathroom carpet and take towel from hook and dry myself; put back towel and step off the curtain, get dressed, put on house slippers
- kitchen: dry dishes with towel, let dishes drip on another towel
- breakfast: sit on a woolen blanket on my upholstered kitchen table chair, leaning lightly on placemat while eating, place cups on coasters
- making the bed
- putting on coat and scarf, twisting around my neck, filling backpack, putting it on right shoulder
- school: sitting on upholstered chair
- cafe: none
- home: putting shoes on carpet near door, drying hands on towel in bathroom and kitchen, sitting chair and leaning on placemat and placing glass of water on coaster; lots of getting on and off the chair; feeling the vibrations of a movie sounds through the placemat and table
- hanging out on top of my bed sheets and woolen blanket (doomscrolling and texting)
- getting into the bed, walking barefoot over the carpet by the side, folding away the blanket to get in, correctly repositioning the bed side lamp shade, leaning with my shoulders on the pillow
- folding away the blanket with one right handed gesture, getting back up walk over two carpets by the bed again and then back again into bed, walking on the bed from the foot of the bed this time 

Textile artifacts interacted with:
- bed: blanket + blanket cover (cotton), pillow + pillowcase (cotton), mattress + bedsheets (cotton), cover blanket (wool)
- lamp: lamp shade
- carpets: 2 next to bed, 1 under lounge chair (did not sit there that day), 1 in bathroom, 1 at each door to outside
- towels: for the entire body (cotton waffle), for hands (cotton frotté), for dishes, for dishes and hands (handwoven cotton kitchen towels)
- shower curtain: textile as a space divider
- placemats and coasters
- chairs: pillows and upholstered chairs (unknown material), blanket on chair (handwoven wool)

- other main interaction was laying and being comfortable
- hours were spent on the upholstered chair at school, mainly only sitting but sometimes also leaning against the back rest
- most textiles are rectangles
