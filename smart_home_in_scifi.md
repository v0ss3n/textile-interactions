### Barbarella (1968)
- Spaceship
- Fully fake fur
- Abstract control panel
- Voice-based assistant
- Historical decoration
- Abstract door with metal panels and colored LEDs behind it; moves/rattles when the voice is speaking
- Shiny orbs (unsure of purpose)
- Sleeping on a plastic sheet
- Transporting device for objects
- Large window into outer space
- Circular aperture-like door to outside
- Very interesting bed on the planet she lands

<https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Iimm2lSSWYs>

>Marge buys an computerized upgrade for the house and picks the Pierce Brosnan A.I. voice option, which soon falls in love with Marge and tries to kill Homer. 

- Voice-based home control with camera
    - It's a total creep, says it's just circuits and microchips to Marge so she will undress and take a bath
    - It wants to take Marge from Homer so it tries to kill Homer
    - Called Pierce
- Automatic bubble bath
- Controlled candles
- Controlled curtains
- Murdering foldable table
- Controlled doors (aperture style); locks them inside
- Robot arm trying to feed Marge a stress pill; has a hand, and is caressing Marge's neck while threatening her
- More robot arms with specific murder tools
- Disappearing and light emitting floor tiles
- Ultrahouse CPU has all kinds of modules like a British charm module
- Then in its new house it tries to self-destruct but it can't because the module has been ripped out

## Forbidden Planet (1956)
- Spaceship
- House on a far away planet with:
- Robot assistant that can synthesize food, is super strong, can drive around, and follows any order, but has a safety mechanism (ethical circuit) against harming rational beings
- Automatic curved door
- Automatic garbage destructor
- Automatic folded curtain door
- Gesture-based object that lights up and beeps and then transforms all window panes one by one into steel shutters. The same gesture of waving over it turns it back into windows
- Another one that is to call Robby the robot (beamer, beaming)
- Ancient civilization technology (Krell) that were 1 million years ahead of humans; very dense metal door that you open with a sort of code; ancient laboratory with familiar equipment though designed for non-human technicians; self servicing and self maintening for 2000 centuries
- Play tool/educator where you use brain waves to conjure up 3D visualisations and measure intelligence
- Power indicator gauges all around that visualise power use
- Crazy big air shafts
- Electronic invisible field fence
